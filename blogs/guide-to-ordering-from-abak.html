04Calculating the Right Quantities for Your Event

One of the most common challenges caterers face is over- or under-ordering protein. Both are costly. Too little and you scramble on the day. Too much and you waste food and money. Use this guide as your starting framework — always adjust for your specific menu and clientele.

General Protein Quantity Guidelines (Per Person, Cooked Portions)

50
50-Guest Event
  • Chicken: 12–15 kg raw
  • Turkey wings: 15–18 kg
  • Goat meat: 10–12 kg bone-in
  • Fish (tilapia/mackerel): 12–14 kg
  • Beef (stew): 8–10 kg
100
100-Guest Event
  • Chicken: 25–30 kg raw
  • Turkey wings: 30–35 kg
  • Goat meat: 20–24 kg bone-in
  • Fish (tilapia/mackerel): 24–28 kg
  • Beef (stew): 16–20 kg
300
300-Guest Event
  • Chicken: 70–85 kg raw
  • Turkey wings: 85–100 kg
  • Goat meat: 60–70 kg bone-in
  • Fish (tilapia/mackerel): 70–80 kg
  • Beef (stew): 48–58 kg
500
500-Guest Event
  • Chicken: 120–140 kg raw
  • Turkey wings: 140–165 kg
  • Goat meat: 100–115 kg bone-in
  • Fish (tilapia/mackerel): 115–135 kg
  • Beef (stew): 80–95 kg
⚠️

Always add a 10–15% buffer: These figures are general guides based on typical Ghanaian event service. Actual consumption varies widely based on menu structure (how many dishes, rice vs fufu), guest demographics, and service style (buffet vs plated). We recommend adding a 10–15% buffer on any order above 100 guests. It is far better to have a small surplus than to run short mid-service.

05Timing Your Order: The Critical Dates

In catering, timing your supply order is not just about convenience — it is a risk management exercise. Here is how to think about your ordering timeline.

  • 7–10 days before the event: Confirm your final menu and calculate your protein quantities. Contact Abak to check stock availability for your required products and quantities.
  • 5–7 days before: Place your confirmed order with Abak, agree the delivery date, and pay your deposit. This is your window of safety for large events (300+ guests).
  • 48–72 hours before: Standard lead time for most bulk orders in Greater Accra. This works well for events up to 200 guests where stock is confirmed in advance.
  • 24 hours before: Minimum lead time we recommend for orders already confirmed. Same-day dispatch is possible for smaller orders confirmed before 11am, but we do not recommend relying on this for catering events.
  • Day of event: Never order for same-day delivery for a catering event. If something goes wrong — traffic, vehicle issues, stock variance — you have no time to recover.
📅

Festive season warning: During peak seasons — Christmas, Easter, Independence Day celebrations, Eid, and end-of-year corporate events — demand for frozen protein in Ghana spikes significantly. During these periods, place your order at least 10–14 days in advance to guarantee your stock. We cannot stress this strongly enough — it is the period when we see the most last-minute panics from caterers who did not plan ahead.

06Receiving & Storing Your Bulk Order

A bulk frozen food delivery is only as good as what happens when it arrives. Here is how to receive your order correctly and store it safely before your event.

At the Point of Delivery

  • Check that the delivery vehicle is refrigerated. All Abak deliveries are made in temperature-controlled vehicles. If the delivery arrives in a non-refrigerated vehicle, query this immediately.
  • Inspect product packaging before signing off. Check for any damaged, torn, or compromised packaging. Note any issues on the delivery receipt before signing.
  • Check the temperature of products. Products should feel solidly frozen — hard to the touch. If products are partially thawed upon arrival, contact our team immediately and document the condition with photos.
  • Count and verify quantities against your order confirmation. Do this before the delivery team departs.

Storage Before Your Event

  • Ensure your freezer capacity is ready before delivery. A common mistake is booking a large delivery without confirming you have the freezer space to accommodate it.
  • Maintain −18°C or below in all storage units. Check your freezer temperature when the delivery arrives and again 2–3 hours later to confirm it has recovered from the loading process.
  • Organise products by type and use date. Keep different protein types separated and labelled clearly to make your prep team's work faster and safer.
  • Begin defrosting in the refrigerator the night before cooking for any products that will be cooked the next day. Never defrost large quantities of protein at room temperature in Ghana's heat.

08Pro Tips from the Abak Supply Team

After working with hundreds of caterers across Ghana, our team has seen what separates the operations that run smoothly from those that struggle. Here are the habits of Abak's most successful catering clients.

  • Build a monthly supply relationship, not a per-event one. Our best clients call us regularly — not just when they have an event. A standing monthly order means we can guarantee your stock, give you priority pricing, and flag any supply issues well in advance.
  • Share your event calendar with us at the start of the month. If we know what you have coming up, we can proactively ensure the right stock is available — even during peak festive seasons when supply gets tight.
  • Always have a secondary protein option in mind. If your client agrees, having a backup protein (e.g. turkey tips if goat is unavailable) gives you flexibility without compromising quality.
  • Invest in adequate freezer capacity. The biggest constraint for growing catering businesses is not money — it is storage. Being able to hold a larger standing stock means you are never caught short before an event.
  • Use WhatsApp for routine orders — call for urgent ones. Our WhatsApp channel is great for standard orders and routine communication. But if you have an urgent, time-sensitive need, always call us directly. Phones get answered faster than messages when every hour counts.
  • Inspect every delivery immediately. Do not wait until cooking day to discover an issue with a delivery. Inspect upon arrival so any problems can be resolved with plenty of time to spare.
