How to Properly Store Frozen Chicken at Home to Keep It Safe & Tasty

Done right, frozen chicken is just as nutritious, just as flavourful, and just as safe as "fresh" — but only if you store it correctly. Most households unknowingly make mistakes that cost them quality, safety, and money. Here's everything you need to know.

Walk into any Ghanaian household and you will almost certainly find frozen chicken somewhere in the kitchen. It is the most popular protein on the continent — and for good reason. It is affordable, versatile, and available year-round. But here is the truth most people do not know: the quality of your frozen chicken has everything to do with how you store it, not just where it comes from.

At Abak Company Limited, we have spent years ensuring that every piece of chicken we supply arrives in perfect condition. But what happens after it leaves our cold storage and lands in your kitchen? That is entirely up to you. This guide gives you the knowledge to protect your investment, keep your family safe, and enjoy the best possible flavour every time.

01Why Temperature Is Everything

The single most important factor in frozen food storage is temperature. Bacteria that cause foodborne illness do not survive or multiply at proper freezing temperatures — but the moment temperatures rise above the safe zone, the risk climbs rapidly.

Temperature Scale
Fridge zone (0–4°C) — short-term only
✓ Safe frozen storage
⚠ Bacteria multiply rapidly
Danger Zone ↑

The Golden Rule of Frozen Chicken Storage

−18°C or below

Ghana's tropical climate means your freezer is working harder than freezers in cooler climates. Ensure your freezer is set to at least −18°C (0°F) — this is the international food safety standard for frozen storage and the temperature at which bacteria effectively stop multiplying.

💡 If your freezer does not have a temperature display, invest in a simple freezer thermometer — they cost very little and can prevent a lot of food waste and illness.

⚠️

Watch out for "warm cycles": Every time you open your freezer door, warm air enters and the temperature temporarily rises. Avoid leaving the freezer open for extended periods, and never place hot or warm food directly into the freezer — let it cool to room temperature first.

02Packaging: The Difference Between Freezer Burn and Perfect Chicken

Many people assume that as long as chicken is frozen, it is fine — regardless of how it is wrapped. This is one of the most costly assumptions in home food storage. Freezer burn — those grey, dry, leathery patches that appear on poorly wrapped chicken — is not caused by the cold, but by exposure to air inside the freezer.

Original Packaging vs. Re-Packaging

Abak chicken arrives in food-grade sealed packaging designed for frozen storage. If you intend to use the entire pack within a few days of purchase, the original packaging is sufficient. However, if you plan to store it for longer, or if you have opened the pack, re-packaging is strongly recommended.

01

Use Freezer-Grade Bags

Regular thin plastic bags are not sufficient. Use heavy-duty, zip-lock freezer bags designed specifically for frozen storage — they are thicker and form a much tighter seal against air.

02

Remove All Air

Press out as much air as possible before sealing the bag. If you have a vacuum sealer, even better — vacuum-sealed chicken can last significantly longer without quality loss.

03

Double-Wrap for Long Storage

For storage beyond 3 months, place the zip-lock bag inside a layer of aluminium foil or a second freezer bag. This extra barrier dramatically reduces freezer burn risk.

04

Portion Before Freezing

Divide chicken into meal-sized portions before freezing. This way you only thaw what you need — avoiding repeated freeze-thaw cycles which degrade both quality and safety.

05

Label Everything

Write the date of freezing clearly on each pack. Frozen chicken can look identical after 3 weeks or 6 months — you cannot tell the difference by sight, so dates are essential.

06

Flat-Freeze for Space

Lay portioned bags flat to freeze. Once solid, they stack neatly like books, making your freezer far more organised and efficient — and portions thaw faster when flat.

03How Long Can You Actually Keep Frozen Chicken?

One of the most common questions we receive is: "How long does frozen chicken last?" The honest answer is that it depends on the type of chicken product and how well it is stored. The table below gives you a reliable guide based on international food safety standards.

Chicken Product Recommended Max. Freezer Time Quality After This Period Safety
Whole chicken (raw) Up to 12 months Good quality if well-packaged Safe
Chicken pieces / cuts (raw) Up to 9 months Good quality; some texture change possible Safe
Chicken mince (raw) 3–4 months Quality degrades faster; use sooner Safe if stored properly
Cooked chicken (leftovers) 2–3 months Texture and flavour may change noticeably Safe
Thawed chicken (uncooked) Do not refreeze without cooking first Significant quality loss; potential bacterial growth Risk if refrozen raw
✅

Important note: "Safe" in the table above assumes storage at a consistent −18°C or below, in proper packaging. After the recommended period, the chicken may still be technically safe to eat, but quality — flavour, texture, moisture — will have noticeably declined. For the best eating experience, use chicken within the recommended window.

"The freezer is not a time machine. Frozen food stops deteriorating — it does not reverse deterioration. Start with great quality, like Abak chicken, and store it correctly from day one." — Abak Food Safety Team

04The Right Way to Defrost Frozen Chicken

How you thaw your chicken is just as important as how you store it. The defrosting stage is where many home cooks unknowingly create food safety risks — particularly by defrosting at room temperature on the counter.

Safe Defrosting Methods

  • ✓
    In the refrigerator (best method): Transfer frozen chicken from the freezer to the fridge the night before you plan to cook it. It will thaw slowly and safely at fridge temperature (0–4°C). Whole chickens may need 24 hours; pieces typically take 12–24 hours.
  • ✓
    In cold running water: Place the sealed chicken pack in a bowl under cold running water. Pieces will thaw in 1–2 hours. Do not use warm or hot water — this raises the surface temperature into the danger zone while the centre is still frozen.
  • ✓
    In the microwave (if cooking immediately): Use the defrost setting and cook the chicken immediately afterwards — never refreeze or leave chicken that has been microwave-defrosted.
  • ✗
    On the kitchen counter (never do this): The outer layers reach dangerous temperatures (above 5°C) while the centre is still frozen. Bacteria multiply rapidly in the warm outer sections. This is a significant food safety risk, especially in Ghana's hot climate.
  • ✗
    In warm or hot water (avoid): Same problem as counter-defrosting — the outside warms too quickly and enters the bacterial danger zone before the inside has thawed.
🚨

Ghana's climate matters here: In Accra's heat, room temperature can easily reach 30–35°C. At these temperatures, bacteria can double in number every 20 minutes on the surface of a thawing chicken. What might be a manageable risk in a cooler climate becomes a serious hazard in Ghana's heat. Always defrost in the fridge or under cold water.

05Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Even experienced home cooks make these mistakes regularly. Go through this checklist honestly — you may find one or two surprises.

  • ✗
    Not checking the freezer temperature regularly. Most home freezers in Ghana run warmer than people think, especially older models. Check yours with a thermometer — it should read −18°C or colder.
  • ✗
    Overloading the freezer. When a freezer is too full, air cannot circulate properly and temperatures become uneven. Some areas of the freezer may not maintain the correct temperature.
  • ✗
    Refreezing thawed raw chicken. Once raw chicken has been fully thawed, it must be cooked before being refrozen. Refreezing thawed raw chicken significantly degrades quality and can create food safety risks if it was not stored properly during thawing.
  • ✗
    Ignoring the "use by" date on the pack. Even frozen chicken has a best-before date. This date assumes the product has been stored correctly at −18°C continuously from packaging to your freezer.
  • ✗
    Storing raw chicken next to cooked foods. Even in a frozen state, always keep raw chicken away from cooked or ready-to-eat items in the freezer to prevent any cross-contamination risk during thawing.
  • ✗
    Losing track of what's in the freezer. A simple list on your freezer door of what's inside and when it was stored can save you from mystery chicken that has been there for 18 months.

06Quick-Reference Summary

📋

The Abak Frozen Chicken Storage Checklist:

✓ Freezer at −18°C or below at all times
✓ Airtight, freezer-grade packaging on all chicken
✓ Portions labelled with date of freezing
✓ Use whole chicken within 12 months, cuts within 9 months
✓ Defrost in the fridge or cold water — never on the counter
✓ Cook thawed chicken within 24 hours of complete thawing
✓ Never refreeze raw thawed chicken without cooking first
✓ Keep raw chicken separate from cooked/ready-to-eat foods
